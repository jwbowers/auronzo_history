# Auronzo di Cadore — a history

A single-page history of **Auronzo di Cadore**, a Ladin-speaking town in the Dolomites (province of Belluno, Veneto, Italy), told from the rock up: how the valley was governed and how its people earned their living, from the first Mesolithic hunters through the timber rafts, the mines, the wars, the emigration, and the eyewear-and-tourism present.

It was made for a reunion of the descendants of Auronzo.

## What's here

The whole site is one self-contained file, `index.html` — no build step, no frameworks, nothing to install beyond two web fonts it loads from Google. GitHub Pages serves it as-is. To read it on your own machine, open `index.html` in any browser.

## How it was made

This history was researched and written with **Claude, the AI assistant made by Anthropic**, working from sources in Italian, German, English, and Ladin — the Magnifica Comunità di Cadore, local archaeology and museum collections, mining and demographic records, and regional histories. Where Auronzo's own record is thin, the text draws on the wider history of Cadore and the Belluno mountains, and says so where it does.

Because an AI assembled it from those sources, it can be wrong. Dates for the deep past are approximate; some figures — the dead on Monte Piana, the toll at Vajont — are the commonly cited estimates; and much of the 20th-century economic and social story is documented at the Cadore and Belluno level and applied to Auronzo.
